MENTAL HEALTH

According to recent statistics one in four students suffer from mental health problems. When to get help: It’s normal to feel down, anxious or stressed from time to time but if these feelings affect your daily activities, including your studies or don’t go away after a couple of weeks, get help. Signs of depression and anxiety include:  Feeling low  Feeling more anxious or agitated than usual  Losing interest in life  Losing motivation There are many support systems available to provide a hand:

Let’s Talk

Let’s Talk is a depression & anxiety service in Hull, they offer CBT and counselling. Contact details: Call 01482 247 111 or Text TALK to 61825

Wellbeing Team

Part of University of Hull, the Wellbeing Team are there to provide a listening ear to support and advise in a safe, confidential and non-judgemental environment. Whether someone is on the autistic spectrum or needs help with the transition to university life, or need to chat about exam the Wellbeing team are available to support your success. Call 01482 462222 Email studentwellbeing@hull.ac.uk

Samaritans offer a safe place to talk any time you wish, about whatever is on your mind. Call 01482 116123 Email jo@samritans.org .

Stay away from any forms of distraction during study periods.
